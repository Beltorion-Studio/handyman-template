# Seasonal Home Preparation: Your Complete Checklist

Each season brings unique challenges for homeowners. By following this comprehensive seasonal preparation checklist, you can protect your home, save money on repairs, and ensure your family's comfort year-round.

## Spring: Renewal and Recovery

After winter's harsh conditions, spring is the perfect time to assess and repair any damage while preparing for warmer weather.

### üå∏ **Exterior Inspection and Maintenance**

**Roof and Gutters**

- [ ] Inspect roof for damaged or missing shingles
- [ ] Check for ice dam damage around eaves
- [ ] Clean gutters and downspouts thoroughly
- [ ] Ensure proper drainage away from foundation
- [ ] Trim tree branches away from roof

**Siding and Foundation**

- [ ] Look for cracks, gaps, or damage in siding
- [ ] Check caulking around windows and doors
- [ ] Inspect foundation for new cracks or settling
- [ ] Clean and seal deck or patio surfaces
- [ ] Power wash exterior surfaces

**Landscaping Preparation**

- [ ] Rake and remove winter debris from lawn
- [ ] Prune damaged branches from trees and shrubs
- [ ] Test and repair irrigation systems
- [ ] Prepare garden beds for planting
- [ ] Check and repair fencing

### üå∏ **Interior Systems Check**

**HVAC Transition**

- [ ] Replace furnace filter
- [ ] Schedule professional HVAC tune-up
- [ ] Test air conditioning before hot weather
- [ ] Clean air vents and registers
- [ ] Check thermostat batteries

**Plumbing Revival**

- [ ] Check for frozen pipe damage
- [ ] Test outdoor spigots and hoses
- [ ] Inspect water heater for leaks or corrosion
- [ ] Clean showerheads and faucet aerators
- [ ] Check toilet tanks for proper function

## Summer: Protection and Efficiency

Summer's heat and storms require preparation for energy efficiency and weather protection.

**Window and Door Efficiency**

- [ ] Install storm doors if needed
- [ ] Check weatherstripping effectiveness

- [ ] Inspect outdoor electrical connections
- [ ] Check surge protector functionality
- [ ] Consider whole-house surge protection
- [ ] Ensure flashlights have fresh batteries

## Fall: Preparation for Winter

Fall preparation is crucial for protecting your home during winter's harsh conditions.

### üçÇ **Winterization Essentials**

**Heating System Preparation**

- [ ] Schedule professional furnace inspection
- [ ] Replace furnace filter
- [ ] Test heating system before cold weather
- [ ] Check and clean fireplace and chimney
- [ ] Stock up on heating fuel if needed

**Plumbing Protection**

- [ ] Drain and store outdoor hoses
- [ ] Shut off outdoor water valves
- [ ] Insulate exposed pipes in unheated areas
- [ ] Check water heater temperature setting
- [ ] Locate and test main water shut-off valve

### üçÇ **Weather Sealing**

**Insulation and Sealing**

- [ ] Check attic insulation levels
- [ ] Seal gaps around windows and doors
- [ ] Install door sweeps if needed
- [ ] Check basement and crawl space insulation
- [ ] Weather-strip around garage doors

**Exterior Preparation**

- [ ] Clean and store patio furniture
- [ ] Drain and winterize irrigation systems
- [ ] Apply winter fertilizer to lawn
- [ ] Plant spring bulbs
- [ ] Check and repair exterior lighting

## Winter: Monitoring and Maintenance

Winter maintenance focuses on monitoring systems and preventing cold-weather damage.

### ‚ùÑÔ∏è **Cold Weather Vigilance**

**Daily Monitoring**

- [ ] Keep thermostat at consistent temperature
- [ ] Open cabinet doors under sinks during freezes
- [ ] Let faucets drip during extreme cold
- [ ] Check for ice dams on roof edges
- [ ] Monitor humidity levels (30-50% ideal)

**Snow and Ice Management**

- [ ] Keep walkways and driveways clear
- [ ] Remove snow from around HVAC units
- [ ] Check for proper ventilation around gas appliances
- [ ] Monitor gutters for ice buildup
- [ ] Keep emergency supplies accessible

**Humidity Control**

- [ ] Use humidifiers to prevent dry air

- [ ] Change furnace filters more frequently
- [ ] Monitor for condensation on windows
- [ ] Check for signs of mold in damp areas

## Creating Your Personal Schedule

**Tips for Success:**

1. **Use a calendar** to schedule seasonal tasks
2. **Break large tasks** into smaller, manageable steps
3. **Combine similar tasks** for efficiency
4. **Keep records** of maintenance dates and findings
5. **Budget for seasonal needs** throughout the year

## When to Call Professionals

While many seasonal tasks are DIY-friendly, some require professional expertise:

- **HVAC system inspections and repairs**
- **Electrical work beyond basic maintenance**
- **Roof repairs or major gutter work**
- **Tree trimming near power lines**
- **Structural repairs or assessments**

## The Investment That Pays Back

Regular seasonal maintenance might seem time-consuming, but it:

- **Prevents costly emergency repairs**
- **Extends the life of home systems**
- **Improves energy efficiency**
- **Maintains home value**
- **Ensures family safety and comfort**

## Start Where You Are

Don't feel overwhelmed by the complete list. Start with:

1. **Safety-critical items** (smoke detectors, heating system)
2. **Weather-urgent tasks** for the current season
3. **High-impact, low-effort items** for quick wins
4. **One room or system at a time** for major projects
